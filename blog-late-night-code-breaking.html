Late Night Code Breaking

I took a break. I always do. Indeterminate, necessary. I paint when I feel it — and when I don’t, I don’t.

But even then, I was building. The site you’re on now? Mine. Every pixel, every button, every late-night curse at broken code. Still part of the mythos. Still forward motion.

The only physical piece I finished in that downtime was this charcoal — a retirement gift for someone from the day job. That weird liminal place where "real life" lives. It came out human. Softer than I felt while drawing it.

Charcoal Portrait – Retirement Gift

But lately... I feel the pull again. The itch. The crawl under the skin that says “pick up the brush.”
