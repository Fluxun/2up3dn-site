Late Night Code Breaking

I took a break. I always do. Indeterminate length. I paint when I feel it, when I don’t, I don’t. But even during downtime, I try to stay orbiting the work. I built this website during one of those quiet spells — my own gallery, by my own hands — every button, every glitch, every late-night curse at broken code. It’s art-adjacent. It’s myth-building. Even if the brush wasn’t wet, the story moved forward.

The only finished piece during that stretch was a charcoal — a retirement gift for someone at my day job. That liminal world where “real life” lives. It came out soft. More human than I felt while making it.
