Late Night Code Breaking

I always take breaks. Indeterminate lengths. I paint when I feel it—when I don’t, I don’t. But even in the off-time, I’m still moving the myth forward. That’s when the site came together. My own gallery, by my own hands—every button, every glitch, every late-night curse at broken code—all of it. It’s art-adjacent. Still the work.

The only physical piece finished during that downtime was a charcoal portrait—a retirement gift for someone at the day job. That liminal space where "real life" lives. It came out soft, human. More human than I felt making it.

It’s summer now. Gallery season. There’s a Black & White show at Art in the Couv I’m eyeing—thinking about submitting a couple charcoals. We’ll see.
