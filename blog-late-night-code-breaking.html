Late Night Code Breaking

I always take breaks. Indeterminate ones. I paint when I feel it. When I don’t, I don’t. But even during those stretches, I’ve never really stepped away. I built this site during one of those painting droughts. My own gallery, by my own hands — every button, every glitch, every late-night curse at broken code — all of it. It’s art-adjacent. It’s me still pushing the myth forward, even if the brush isn’t wet.

The only physical piece I finished during that time was this charcoal — a retirement gift for a friend at the day job. That liminal world where “real life” lives.

I think it came out alright. Soft. Respectful. More human than I felt while making it.

But the itch is back. That hum in the gut that says: *time to finish things.* These are what I’m circling now:
